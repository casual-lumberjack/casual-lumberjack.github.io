Atlas Book Production

This project required the conceptualization and physical design of a multi-page atlas showcasing advanced terrain visualizations. Executed primarily in Adobe InDesign, the objective was to master commercial publishing fundamentals, bridging the gap between digital cartographic generation and physical book production.


The resulting 9"x6" book synthesizes complex spatial rasters (LIDAR, Imhof relief, false color enhancements) into a cohesive, press-ready document. The workflow demanded strict adherence to commercial print specifications, including parent page management, precise bleed settings, and automated pagination.

Multi-Page Layout Architecture
Engineered facing-page spreads utilizing InDesign Parent Pages to ensure consistent pagination and structural layout.
Guarantees document uniformity and drastically reduces error rates in lengthy publishing projects.
Pre-Press Raster Management
Processed high-resolution terrain imagery, enforcing strict 220 ppi standards at 100% scale without interpolation.
Ensures critical topographic details remain sharp and legible when subjected to physical offset printing.
Typographic Hierarchy & Consistency
Implemented structured Character and Paragraph Styles to enforce typographic discipline across all descriptive text.
Allows for global formatting adjustments and maintains a professional, cohesive reading experience.
Commercial Print Specification
Configured precise trims (9"x6"), 0.25" bleeds, and asymmetrical margins to accommodate physical plasticomb binding.
Prevents critical map data from being lost to the physical binding process or page trimming.
Cartographic Packaging
Compiled final working files into a structured InDesign Project Package, preserving all fonts, links, and high-res assets.
Ensures seamless handoff to commercial printing facilities without risk of missing critical map layers.

Visualizing Terrain & 3D Representations

The Visualization of Terrain Relief Labs represent a multi-week exploration into the physical and digital representation of topography to create a wide variety of cartographic outputs. The core objective was to transcend flat, uninspiring aesthetics of default GIS hillshading by manually manipulating light, shadow, and elevation data across ArcGIS Pro, Adobe Photoshop, and Blender.

Advanced methodologies included generating grayscale outputs with varying azimuth and altitude, utilizing specific blending modes (like Difference) to extract deep shadows, and employing posterization for illuminated ridgelines. The workflow also extended to manipulating 16-bit DEM heightmaps in open-source CGI environments for cinematic atmospheric rendering, and utilizing raw LiDAR point clouds (LAZ files) with binning interpolation to map hyper-localized relief.

Composite Hillshading Mastered the integration of ArcGIS Pro and Adobe Photoshop to build multi-layered composite hillshades, strategically manipulating transparency and blending modes. Elevates scientific elevation data into visually stunning, hyper-realistic terrain maps that communicate elevation intuitively.
Feature Extraction Applied advanced image processing techniques to isolate shadow layers via difference blending and utilized posterization to generate ridgeline highlights. Allows absolute artistic control over the landscape, selectively emphasizing structural geology or masking irrelevant topography.
CGI Terrain Rendering Leveraged Blender to generate cinematic shaded relief, applying complex illumination arrays, surface texturing, and non-color data processing. Introduces photorealistic atmospheric effects, ray-traced shadows, and ambient occlusion to 2D map design.
LiDAR Processing Executed end-to-end processing of high-resolution LiDAR data, utilizing binning interpolation to generate precision DEMs. Proves ability to handle massive point-cloud datasets and extract usable surface models for localized analysis.
Illumination Analysis Demonstrated deep understanding of lighting theory, deliberately altering altitude and azimuth parameters to model effects on continuous topographic surfaces. Ensures simulated sun angles correctly emphasize terrain features without creating optical illusions like relief inversion.

Spatial-Temporal Animation

This project explored the visualization of geographic change over time, requiring the creation of a compelling cartographic animation in ArcGIS Pro. The objective was to move beyond static mapping to effectively illustrate trends, movement, and data phenomena over time.


The workflow required rigorous preparation of time-enabled feature layers, careful selection of coordinate reference systems, and the configuration of animation keyframes. Pacing, frame rates, and dynamic text overlays were precisely engineered to engage viewers without causing visual fatigue.


The dataset chosen for this project was sourced from ACLED and focused on civil unrest in North America from 2019-2026. Viewed month by month, the animation reveals the cyclical nature of civil unrest in line with the election cycle, as well as flashpoints where definitive events shape the course of history.

Time-Enabled Visualization
Configured temporal parameters on feature layers to animate geospatial phenomena across specific intervals.
Reveals hidden temporal patterns, velocities, and movement that static maps cannot convey.
Keyframe Synchronization
Engineered smooth transitions, pacing, and frame rates to optimize viewer engagement.
Prevents viewer fatigue and ensures complex changes over time are easily digestible.
Temporal Data Structuring
Cleaned and formatted datasets to ensure strict compatibility with ArcGIS Pro's time slider mechanics.
Guarantees accurate sequential rendering of data frames without technical errors.
Dynamic Layout Design
Integrated dynamic text elements, including self-updating time stamps and customized contextual legends.
Provides critical contextual wayfinding for the viewer throughout the animation lifecycle.
Multimedia Export Optimization
Exported finalized sequences into optimized MP4 and GIF formats maintaining high cartographic fidelity.
Ensures the final product is highly accessible and easily shared across digital platforms.

Oblique Panorama

The Oblique Panorama project stands as the capstone evaluation for the Visualizing Terrain course with a postcard style view of Western Brook Pond in Gros Morne National Park. Diverging radically from traditional orthographic mapping, this project required the creation of highly realistic, oblique-view terrain maps simulating a physical camera lens interacting with a vast geographic landscape.


The complexity is defined by its rigorous, multi-software pipeline. Massive environmental datasets (Landsat, LandCover, DEMs) were routed through ArcGIS Pro, Blender, and Photoshop before being integrated into Adobe Illustrator. The final output is an expansive, wide-format composition demonstrating a seamless fusion of spatial data processing, 3D visualization, and commercial graphic design.

Raster Management Integrated massive environmental datasets, ensuring outputs met strict 220 ppi print production standards. Ensures multi-gigabyte spatial imagery does not pixelate when subjected to offset printing.

Vector Integration Engineered press-ready layouts in Illustrator, overlaying precise vector networks, typography, and basemap features. Synthesizes the realism of photographic rasters with the mathematical precision of vector cartography.

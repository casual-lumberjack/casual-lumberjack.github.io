Flow Maps: Visualizing Statistical Migration

The Flow Maps assignment serves as a rigorous, foundational introduction to statistical mapping techniques, specifically targeting the complex challenge of visualizing spatial movement, migration, and flux. Executed during the early weeks of the Winter 2026 semester, this project is intricately mapped to Learning Outcomes 2 and 4 of the Thematic Cartography curriculum.

The cartographic challenge required the visualization of quantitative data, in this case Werner Butterfly migration paths, over geographic space. A critical constraint placed upon this workflow was the strict prohibition of default, pre-packaged basemaps. This ensured that factors such as scale and requisite level of detail were considered in the manual sourcing and customization of independent vector basemap content, ensuring the underlying geography provided context without obfuscating the statistical flow lines. The final product was executed entirely within Adobe Illustrator, translating raw geographical outputs into clean, press-ready vector files.

2D Flood Hazard Modeling: Stewiacke River

Hydrodynamic Simulation & Vulnerability Assessment

Executive Summary

Serving as the capstone for the Applied Thematic Project, this model addresses the complex hydrodynamic hazards of the Stewiacke River. Driven by climate change and sea-level rise, the confluence of downstream river flow and upstream Bay of Fundy tides requires advanced two-dimensional (2D) simulation rather than traditional flat-water 1D mapping to accurately predict municipal flood vulnerabilities.

The technical workflow bridged geospatial design and civil engineering. It involved synthesizing 1-meter LiDAR, soil infiltration data, and tidal boundaries in ArcGIS Pro, followed by statistical frequency analysis in HEC-SSP. The final 15-meter calculation mesh was iteratively debugged and simulated in HEC-RAS 6.6, producing critical thematic maps intended for emergency response and municipal town planning.

Vulnerability Assessment Findings

The successful completion of the HEC-RAS 2D simulation yielded a comprehensive 100-year predictive flood model (adjusted for anticipated mean sea level rise and peak astronomical tides). The outputs highlighted several distinct vulnerability zones requiring immediate municipal attention:

1. Regional Logistical Isolation

The model indicates that both northbound and southbound routes on Highway 102 and Highway 2 become completely flooded. The town effectively becomes an island, severely straining local municipal resources and necessitating reliance on internal emergency response capabilities.

2. Secondary Wastewater Hazards

Three municipal sewage settling ponds are located directly within the primary flood zone. When the river breaches its banks, these facilities will introduce hazardous biological waste into the floodwaters, elevating the risk profile to a hazardous materials scenario.

3. Severe Residential Inundation

The Low Tide Road Trailer Park presents the most critical vulnerability. The model projects dwellings will be submerged in up to 1.16 meters of water, creating a serious risk of buoyancy. Furthermore, all access roads to this park become fully inundated, severely hindering evacuation and rescue efforts.

Project Lifecycle & Resilience

Beyond technical execution, the project lifecycle relied on rigorous collaboration, including weekly peer critiques, design sample submissions, and structured workplans. A draft iteration was subjected to client review, ensuring the final emergency mapping series strictly aligns with municipal planning requirements and creates a resilient, reproducible framework for future hazard assessments.

Core Competencies

Advanced Thematic Design

Transforms raw simulation output into highly legible, actionable thematic maps for municipal policymakers, meeting precise cartographic publishing standards.

Hydrodynamic Simulation (HEC-RAS)

Evaluated and implemented industry-standard software to calculate the physics of water depth, speed, and directional flow over a continuous 2D grid.

Geospatial Data Engineering

Synthesized disparate datasets (WebTide ocean data, historical WSC river flows, continuous surface friction codes) within ArcGIS Pro to create an accurate digital twin of the landscape.

Iterative Python Automation

Employed Python scripting to automate culvert data entry and resolved critical FORTRAN array misalignments, demonstrating advanced technical troubleshooting.
